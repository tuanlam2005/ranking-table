Deforestation
is one of the most significant contributors to climate change. Deforestation and forest degradation contribute to atmospheric greenhouse gas emissions through combustion of forest biomass and decomposition of remaining plant material and soil carbon.
Gas emissions
from human activities are one of the leading causes of climate change since the mid-20th century. These gases don't escape the atmosphere but they build up and trap heat in the atmosphere, leading to global warming.
As a result, melting glaciers
in the polar regions are causing sea levels to rise, submerging coastal areas, endangering people living in such area. Furthermore, the melting of glaciers could potentially unleash ancient viruses and bacteria that have been dormant for thousands of years, for which we have no prevention against.
